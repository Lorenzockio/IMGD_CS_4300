My shader consists of two components that change over time, a distance field and a sin wave. The distance field had three forms. 
The first two expand and contract using a sin() oscilator called pulse. One is shaped using sin() and the other uses cos(). The third form 
expands infinitely based on seconds(). I manipulated these fields by changing the origin to be in a corner or in the center of the screen.
My line is not truly a sin wave as I used tan(sin()) to have higher peaks. The wave is affected by audio[2]. I used a virtual cable to connect
my headphone output to the input. This way I can use music instead of a microphone. I also utilized lastframe to create interesting feedback
in two ways. One uses mix() and the other uses x + feedback * .y. 

Aesthetically, I wanted everything to feel connected and flow together. I used PI to time the distance field transitions so that the origin
only moved when the screen was black. When I manually changed things with comments, I tried to sync up with the music and current visuals like
a DJ mixing music. One example is when I transition to a infinitely expanding distance field at a drop. For colors, I used a red background 
mainly because I liked how the distance fields looked with black and red. My line is purple because it combines well with the red while still 
standing out on its own. Looking closely you can see artifacts of red trailing offof the line. 

All in all, I am proud of this as my first live code. I didn't get much feedback, but some people said they liked my transitions and my ending 
fade which made me feel validated. Being self critical, I find some sections a little repetive and boring. In the last 30-40 seconds I didn't 
make any real changes (I blame OBS for ruining previous takes). I probably should have picked a shorter song.
